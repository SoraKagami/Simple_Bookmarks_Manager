### Recommended approach

Use **short-lived per-render/per-operation maps only**, not a persistent cache.

Example:

- Load fresh bookmark tree with `chrome.bookmarks.getTree()`.
- Build temporary maps:

    - `id -> node`
    - `id -> parent`
    - `id -> children`
- Use them only during that render or operation.
- Discard them after the operation completes.

This improves readability and reduces repeated recursive walking **without** creating a long-term stale-cache risk.

```
Global bookmarkTreeCacheGlobal idToNode map reused across timeGlobal parentMap reused after create/delete/move/update
```
